Yolk

Finding local businesses based on the values you care about.

Problem

The current state of the local business review sites has focused primarily on user-generated star ratings. What the existing solution fails to address is the reliability of reviews and consumer values. Our product will address this gap by focusing on the value systems of local businesses. Our initial focus will be political consumers to easily find and purchase products/services that align with their values, eliminating research time and confusion.

Research

Our primary and secondary research indicate that there is a cohort of millennials that are not engaging in politics in a traditional way by voting in elections, but are rather engaging in non-traditional ways like “voting with their dollar.” These millennials, known as “political consumers,” are very critical about where their dollar is going and how that dollar is affecting society at large. They ask themselves questions like, “If I buy coffee today, how does it affect the greater society at large?”, or “If I buy coffee today, how does it affect the village where the coffee came from?” Our secondary research tells us that there is a great need for these consumers to buy products that align with their values. They ask themselves, “How can I buy this product without compromising what I believe in?”

Those political consumers have also a difficulty trusting product/businesses reviews on platforms such as Yelp that are rated based on only the quality of product/service regardless of the values or the process behind those products/services.We also found out in our interviews that political consumers do research online before they buy products at specific businesses and that they are often overwhelmed by the sheer amount of information available to them. They feel that there is information overload online in the area of consumption, making it difficult limited to purchase products that truly align with their values.

Persona


Patricia, 25
A political consumer

We conducted four interviews with local political consumers aged between 25 and 38, and identified our persona as Patricia. Patricia is a political consumer who lives in Toronto. She is educated at bachelor-level and works as a media freelancer. Patricia describes herself as minimalist, committed, ethical and environmental.

She Says
  • I want to be mindful when I shop.
  • I only buy the things that I need.
  • I want to be personally connected to the things that I buy.
  • I want what I buy to be a reflection of who I am and what I believe in.
She Does
  • Routinely shops for grocery and food items on a weekly basis.
  • Only buys what she really needs.
  • She shops locally, with an environmental focus.
  • She uses social media and the internet to get information about products and share information.
  • She researches products and/or companies before she shops, but also values spontaneity, doesn’t mind being surprised or disappointed. She is open to new things.
She Thinks
  • She is contributing to the greater good when she buys or doesn’t buy things.
  • She thinks that she is practicing awareness when she does her homework (research) about the products that she buys.
She Feels
  • Constrained and limited in her ability to buy based on her values.
  • Overwhelmed with the massive amounts of information she receives on a daily basis about the things that she should buy.
  • Skeptical about the information that is available to her.
  • Even with all of these barriers, still feels compelled to buy products based on her values.

Requirements Analysis

As we explored the day of searching for the right coffee shop in Patricia’s shoes, we came face-to-face with her struggles on a more granular level. We made these pain points, our guidelines for solutions. We also interviewed the local businesses to understand their viewpoint in this consumption process and found that though they didn’t have time and/or budget for marketing, they were passionate about the values their businesses stood for and were always looking for customers who connected with it.

As-Is Scenario

In Assessing Needs, the user spend more or less time assessing the needs depending on the item/service he is looking for. In Searching stage, the user finds it easy to gather quantitative information such as price, proximity, but the information about core value is hard to find.

Needs
  1. Patricia needs to find products/businesses that align with her values.
  2. Patricia needs to find well organized information about products/businesses.
  3. Patricia needs to verify the validity of information in order to make informed decisions.
  4. Patricia needs to contribute to the community by reviewing validity of the inform.

Solution

For the To-be scenario, we are focusing solving pain points identified in the search/deciding phase of the as-is scenario by introducing our solution to Patricia’s search.

To-Be Scenario

Patricia is a political consumer who has always tries to practice ethical consumption. She has recently come across the concept of fairtrade coffee and wants to find a place that serves and sells fairtrade coffee.

  1. Our platform enables users wants to search local businesses based on her values using tags.
  2. Our visualization of values enables users to quickly find out what values are represented at the place.
  3. Our platform enables users to share good experience with others using tags that help more people find the place.

Prototypes

Sketches

We used various stages of sketches to brainstorm ideas within the team. This method encouraged exploration of various ideas and helped us develop a shared understanding of the platform.

Creating sequences of screens allowed us to design smoother user flows and visualize the experience the user will have with the app.

Clickable Medium Fidelity Prototype

Having established the basic structure of the app we proceeded with medium-fidelity prototyping using Figma. At this stage, we were able to focus more on the user experience and flow of the app.

High Fidelity Prototype

At this stage, I was able to apply my design skills and knowledge and establish branded design elements. Here we focused on the minute elements of the user experiments. Ensuring the labels, color treatment and styles were consistent throughout the app.

Evaluation

After creating our clickable medium-fidelity prototype, we conducted a summative evaluation for our product. All participants found the Searching and Tagging functionality to be useful as they currently have to read through quite a few qualitative reviews before finding such information. During the search, participants also found the Learn functionality helpful. They also expected to see the value tags they searched in the search results list view as the tags are the USP of the platform. Search functionality on Learn page is also suggested.
