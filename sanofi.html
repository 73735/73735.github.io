DUPIXENT® Parient Support Prograam

Helping atopic dermatitis patients find the right treatment.

Background

Dupixent, is a monoclonal antibody used for allergic diseases such as atopic dermatitis, asthma, and nasal polyps which result in chronic sinusitis.

Due to the prohibition of D2C advertising for prescription-required medications, Sanofi was in need of a PSP (Patient Support Program) s part of user acquisition strategy.

In Japan, the high cost of medications not covered by insurance is also a factor hindering their widespread use.

Challenges

Primary Research

In addition to the secondary research conducted by Sanofi's global marketing team, we conducted 60-minute online interviews with eight AD (Atopic Dermatitis) patients, asking detailed questions about their treatment experiences and daily lives (screening target: 50 people).

We also conducted 60-minute online interviews with two outpatient dermatologists from large hospitals and private practices, discussing the challenges in their clinical practice (screening target: 10 people).

Additionally, we interviewed one PX (Patient Experience representative), two MRs (Medical Representatives), two PSLs (Patient Support Liaisons), and one MSL (Medical Science Liaison) about their daily work, the difficulties they face, and their interactions with patients and doctors.
