Catalina

In-Store Coupon Customer Journey Map.

Background

Catalina is a leader in the distribution of paper-based in-store coupons. As they make a transition from analog to digital, a high-level customer journey map of their in-store coupons was required in order to brainstorm the next step.

Presales

The objective was to create a high-level customer journey map to help value alignment: building empathy, providing a common big picture, breaking silos, reducing complexity, and finding opportunities.

Engagement Goals
  • View Catalina’s offerings from outside-in rather than inside-out.
  • Align internal functions across teams and levels.
  • Create visualizations as shared references.
  • Promote Design Thinking at Catalina.

Customer Value Chain

First, we mapped how Catalina creates and delivers the value to end-users. Although Catalina’s revenue does not directly come from end-users, we decided to map customers’ experience at retail stores at a high level. Providing a positive customer experience will expand Catalina’s retail store network resulting in higher print volume.

Then, we decided whom we should interview within the organization to better understand the role, function, and touchpoints.

Participants
  • CPG Maker Sales
  • Retail Store Sales
  • Retail Analytics

Proto-Personas

We created several proto-personas based on Catalina's knowledge of their customers and secondary research of Japanese shoppers who might come in contact with Catalina's coupons.

  • Users of the retail stores where Catalina has their printers installed.
  • Users of the target products.

We mainly focused on income, shopping frequency, membership status at retail stores. We did not need to go into too much detail as everyone is a shopper and we are not targeting a niche segment.

Interviews

Using an interview screener focusing on demographics, shopping frequency, and familiarity with in-store coupons, we found six participants closely matching our proto-personas.

Structured Questions

We prepared structured questions using an epistemological inventory in order to cover all of our assumptions.

Keywords

We asked interview participants to feely list words associated with in-store coupons.

Ground Tour

We asked interview participants to walk us through the experience of shopping in its entity including their routine before and after the shopping.

Draft Map

Point of View

We discovered oall shoppers will fall in the spectrum between Exploratory Shopper and Target-oriented Shopper. Hence, we decided to use the spectrum for mapping shopper’s actions, thoughts, and feelings.

Exploratory Shopper

  • Lives in suburbs
  • Large basket size
  • Seeks low price
  • Plans in advance

Target-oriented Shopper

  • Lives in inner-city
  • Small basket size
  • Seeks high quality
  • Shops spontaneously
Scope

In order to capture the entire experience of acquiring and using a coupon, we set the scope of the journey to include 3 stages

  • Acquiring a coupon
  • In-between visits
  • Redeeming a coupon
Organization

Based on the customer value chain, we decided to map the operation and goal of three organizations.

  • Retailer
  • Catalina
  • CPG Maker
Interaction

We included touch points and moments of truth not only for coupons, but also for points and target products encountered by the customers in the journey.

  • Coupons
  • Points
  • Target products
Opportunity

We also included opportunities in the form of ‘How Might We’ questions from which solutions can be brainstormed.

  • Insight: Remembering the coupon is hard
  • HMW Make coupons memorable?
  • Insight: Coupons are recognised as ads.
  • HMW Remind customers that coupons are as valuable as money?
  • Insight: Redeeming a coupon is intimidating.
  • HMW Make coupon-redeeming experience more exciting?
  • Insight: Addictive products (with shorter usage cycles) are more suitable for coupons.
  • HMW Make products with longer usage cycles more coupon friendly?
